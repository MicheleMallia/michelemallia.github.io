Hello!

My name is Michele Mallia, I am almost 30 years old and I was born in Palermo but I have always lived in Agrigento. At the age of 19 I moved to Pisa to begin my Digital Humanities studies at the University of Pisa.

In 2015 I obtained my bachelor's degree with a thesis in Computational Linguistics entitled "Spelling correction applied to historical texts of the First World War". Later I continued my studies in Digital Humanities specializing in Graphics, Virtual Environments and Interactivity.

In 2018 I obtained my master's degree with an experimental thesis entitled "Automatic Creation of a Virtual / Augmented Gallery Based on User Defined Queries on Online Public Repositories", whose work was published in a magazine published by Springer.

From 2018 onwards I started working at various companies located between Pisa and Turin as a front-end developer. From 2020 I became a research fellow at the CNR of Pisa and I was included in the PRIN Italia Antica project, whose purpose is to create a platform for the creation of lexicons derived from epigraphic materials to be exported and made available to the scientific community.

In these years I have learned various programming languages ​​and computer technologies such as Angular, TypeScript, Python, Css, Docker and many others.

In addition to work, I like making music, listening to music vinyls, taking photographs with the reflex camera, taking long walks in the mountains, cycling, being around people, traveling and visiting museums.
